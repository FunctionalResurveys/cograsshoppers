Key to Juvenile Grasshoppers

Melanoplus lakinus (Scudder)

Illustration by Ali Moore

 




Melanoplus lakinus (the Lakin grasshopper) is a short winged flightless grasshopper distributed largely through central and western North America. It is often found in hot, dry and open areas with low stature forbs.

Distribution in Colorado: < 1,800m (< 6,000 Ft.)

Hatch Date: Late group, early summer

Body and head color vary between green and tan. Early instars may appear very shiny and blackish. The sides of the pronotum may display a faint white horizontal stripe.

The compound eye is brown with one black stripe crossing the entire eye and two, thinner, yellow stripes bordering it.

A yellow post ocular band is commonly displayed.

The hind femur face has two common appearances; a) there may not be a stripe at all or b) there may 2-3 faint diagonal grey bars.

The hind tibia is blue/green.

 

For more information see M. lakinus Species Fact Sheet.

 

 

Commonly Confused With:

1) M. packardii – M. packardii does not display a crescent on the sides of the pronotum as M. lakinus does. M. packardii has many black spots/speckles on the femur face whereas M. lakinus may have a plain femur face or display 2-3 very faint grey bars. M. packardii lacks an eye stripe whereas M. lakinus has multiple eye stripes. M. packardii hatch earlier and can be found at much higher elevations.
