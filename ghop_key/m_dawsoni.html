Key to Juvenile Grasshoppers

Melanoplus dawsoni (Scudder)

Illustration by Ali Moore

 




Photos by Gerald M. Fauske

Melanoplus dawsoni (the Dawson grasshopper) is a small short-winged montane species that ranges downward to the upper edge of the planes near the foothills. In Colorado it is a rare resident at 1,500 meters but has occasionally been found higher up.

Distribution in Colorado: 1,500 -3,050 m (5,000 – 10,000 Ft)

Hatch Date: Intermediate group, late spring

Body and head have distinct black regions offset by white lateral and bright yellow ventral regions. Most of the head is black with the exception of a white/yellow horizontal stripe on the sides of the pronotum and a white/yellow triangular marking on the posterior part of the lateral lobe.

The compound eye is black throughout and lacks a stripe.

A post ocular band is absent on this species.

The hind femur face has a solid black stripe.

For more information see the M. dawsoni Species Fact Sheet.

 

 

 

Commonly Confused With:

1) M. femerrubrum – M. femerrubrum has much less black on the head whereas, M. dawsoni almost has a completely black head. M. dawsoni has a completely black eye and M. femerrubrum has a two-toned eye, but both lack a stripe. M. femerrubrum hatches later in the season and occurs at lower elevations. Both have yellow undersides. M. dawsoni often show distinctly white lateral regions while M. femurrubrum do not.

2) M. keeleri – M. keeleri has a considerably larger portion of white/cream/yellow on the lower half of the head and pronotum than M. dawsoni. M. keeleri has a distinctive double yellow stripe on the eye, it hatches later and is found at lower elevations than M. dawsoni.
