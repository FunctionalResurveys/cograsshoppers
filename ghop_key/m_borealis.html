Key to Juvenile Grasshoppers

Melanoplus borealis (Fieber)

Illustration by Ali Moore

 




Melanoplus borealis (the northern grasshopper), inhabits a wide range of North America, from Colorado to Alaska. This species inhabits both lowland and mountain sites and inhabits a variety of habitats such as arctic tundra, wet bogs, swamps and moist mountain meadows.

Distribution in Colorado: 3,200 -3,900 m (10,500 – 13,000 Ft)

Hatch Date: Late group, hatch in early summer

Body and head color vary between brown, green, and tan. The sides of the pronotum are marked with a horizontal white stripe which is bordered by black spots/speckles.

The compound eye is tan/brown with the top half being lighter in color than the bottom half. The eyes lack a solid stripe.

A black post ocular band is commonly displayed.

The hind femur face has a solid black stripe and occasionally there will be a small but distinct nick at the distal end.

The hind tibia is normally green or tan.


The hind tibia black/brown/tan in early instars but turns to a blue/green as instars progress.

For more information see the M. borealis Species Fact Sheet.

 

 

 


Commonly Confused With:

1) M. bivitattus – M. bivitattus displays a femur stripe that only occupies the upper medial portion of the hind femur whereas M. borealis have most of the hind femur face covered by the stripe. M. bivittatus hatch in early spring whereas M. borealis hatches early in the summer. M. borealis are found at higher elevations than M. bivittatus.

2) M. fasciatus ( when the femur stripe is NOT broken) – M. fasciatus have a notch at the proximal end of the femur stripe and M. borealis do not display this notch. M. fasciatus hatches in mid-spring whereas, M. borealis hatches in early summer.
