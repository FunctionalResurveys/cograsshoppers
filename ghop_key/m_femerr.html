Key to Juvenile Grasshoppers

Melanoplus femerrubrum (DeGeer)

Illustration by Ali Moore

 




Photos by Gerald M. Fauske

Melanoplus femerrubrum (the red legged grasshopper) is widely distributed throughout most of North America and Mexico. It is the most widely distributed species of the major crop grasshoppers. Its can be found in tall vegetation of grasslands, meadows, crop borders, reverted fields. This species favors low moist weedy areas where its host plants are found.

Distribution in Colorado: <1,500 – 2,100 m (<5,000 – 7000 Ft)

Hatch Date: Intermediate to late group, hatching occurs over an extended period; from late May to July

Body and head have distinct black regions offset by white and yellow regions. A dark vertical stripe runs between the eyes and two to three stripes of white or bright yellow.

The compound eye is brown with the top half being lighter in color than the bottom half. The eye lacks a stripe

A black post ocular band is commonly displayed.

The hind femur face has a solid black stripe.

The hind tibia is pale yellow.

 

For more information see M. femerrubrum Species Fact Sheet.

 

 

Commonly Confused With:

1) M. dawsoni – M. dawsoni has a completely black head where the front and top of the head of M. femerrubrum is not black. M. dawsoni hatches earlier and is found at higher elevations than M. femmurubrum. M. dawsoni also have dark black eyes and, when seen from the top, often have white/yellow stripes that run across the black abdomen.

2) M. keeleri – M. keeleri usually has a largely white region on the lower part of the face and pronotum whereas, M. femerrubrum has numerous black regions. M. keeleri has a distinctive double yellow stripe on the eye and appears at the same elevations as M. femerrubrum.
