Key to Juvenile Grasshoppers

Melanoplus dodgei (Thomas)

Illustration by Ali Moore

 




Melanoplus dodgei (Dodge's Grasshopper) is a flightless short winged species that is restricted to the Rocky Mountain regions of Colorado, Southern Wyoming and Northern New Mexico. It is the most common montane species found in Colorado and is found in open and shady stands of mixed herbaceous vegetation.

Distribution in Colorado: 2,000 - 4,000 m (6,700 – 13,000 Ft)

Hatch Date: Intermediate group, hatching in mid-May at lower sites to mid-June at the higher sites.

Body and head color vary between black, brown, and grey with a heavily molted/spotted appearance. The pronotum lacks distinctive characters.

The compound eye may be brown throughout, or if apparent, lighter brown on top and darker on bottom.

A black post ocular band is commonly displayed.

The hind femur face lacks a stripe but has distinctive dark, unattached chevrons.

The hind tibia is normally the same general color as the body color in this species.

 

For more information see M. dodgei the Biology of Common Colorado Grasshoppers.
 

 

 

Commonly Confused With:

M. dodgei have distinctive chevrons on their femur and are darkly molded. First instars are black and later instars develop a drab grey body color.
