Key to Juvenile Grasshoppers

Melanoplus bivitattus (Say)

Illustration by Ali Moore

 




Photos by Gerald M. Fauske

Melanoplus bivitattus (the two striped grasshopper) is commonly found throughout North America and is one of the largest Melanoplus species. It is closely related to M. differentalis and they share many physical traits. Although these species are found in similar habitats, they are usually temporally separated.

Distribution in Colorado: 1,500 – 3,050 m (5,000 – 10,000 Ft)

Hatch Date: Early group, hatch in mid-spring (as early as May)

Body and head color can vary between green, yellow and tan. The sides of the pronotum are marked with a horizontal white stripe which is bordered by black spots/speckles. 

The compound eye is tan throughout with light spots/speckles. Eye lacks a stripe.

A black post ocular band is commonly displayed, but may be absent.

The hind femur face has a solid black stripe.

The hind tibia is normally green or tan.
 

For more information see the M. bivitattus Species Fact Sheet.

 

 

 

Commonly Confused With:

1) M. differentalis — M. differentalis body color is generally darker brown and more molted/speckled than M. bivaiattus. M. differentalis also have an eye stripe whereas M. bivitattus do not. While found inhabiting similar lowland environments, M. differentalis hatch in late spring after M. bivitattus has already hatched. Later M. differentalis juveniles have leg stripes that turn into chevrons.

2) M. borealis — M. borealis have most of the hind femur face covered by the stripe whereas M. bivitattus displays a femur stripe that only occupies the upper medial portion of the hind femur. M. borealis hatches early in the summer whereas M. bivitattus hatch in early spring. M. boralis are found at much higher elevations than M. bivitattus.

3) M. fasciatus (when the stripe is unbroken) — M. fasciatus has the lower half of the eye darker than the top, M. bivitattus eye is solid throughout. M. fasciatus hatches in mid-spring and generally at higher elevations.

4) P. nebrascensis — P. nebrascensis has a larger head and a black stripe between the eye and mouthparts. P. nebrascensis has a late hatch date and is found at lower elevations. M. bivitattus is larger.
