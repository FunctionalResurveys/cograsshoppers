Key to Juvenile Grasshoppers

Melanoplus confusus (Scudder)

Illustration by Ali Moore

 




photos by Gerald M. Fauske

Melanoplus confusus (the little pasture grasshopper) commonly inhabits lowland prairies, meadows and pastures. In Colorado, it is the earliest Melanoplus species to hatch and reach adulthood.

Distribution in Colorado: 420 – 2,750 m (1,400 – 9,000 Ft)

Hatch Date: Early spring hatching

Body and head color vary between brown, green, and tan. The sides of the pronotum are marked with a horizontal white stripe which may not be apparent in later instars. Often, a black horizontal stripe/spots will occupy the area of the pronotum just above the white horizontal stripe. A dorsal view of the pronotum shows a straight sulcus.

The compound eye is brown with a dark stripe across most of it. Two white stripes border either side of the black stripe.

A black post ocular band is commonly displayed

The hind femur face has a broken black stripe with a notch at the proximal end.

The hind tibia is normally the same general color as the body color in this species.

For more information see the M. confusus Species Fact Sheet.

 

 

Commonly Confused With:

1) M. sanguinipes – M. sanguinipes only has half a stripe on the eye, not a full stripe like M. confusus. M. sanguinipes males have a distinct m shaped notch on the tip of the subgeniteal plate and M. confusus do not. M. sanguinipes have a notch in the sulcus when looking at the dorsal portion of the pronotum and M. confusus have a straight sulcus. While often found at similar lowland sites, M. sanguinipes hatch dates follow the hatch dates of M. confusus.

2) M. fasciatus – M. fasciatus does not have a eye stripe like M. confusus. M. fasciatus inhabit higher elevations and hatch later than M. confusus.
