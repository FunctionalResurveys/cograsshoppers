Alexander’s Main Collecting Sites

The Gordon Alexander collection is composed of over 24,000 grasshoppers from the Rocky Mountain and plains regions of Colorado that were collected from the 1930's to the 1960's. In total, the grasshoppers from the Alexander Collection come from nearly 1,000 unique localities.

During the years of 1958-1960, under the direction of Dr. Alexander and John Hilliard and with the support of the National Science Foundation, grasshoppers were surveyed along an altitudinal gradient in Colorado. The eastern portion of this transect began in the Front Range near Boulder and extended westward over a vertical range of more than 2,000 m; from 1,530 m above sea level in the high plains to 3,660 m above sea level in the western alpine region known as Niwot Ridge (Alexander and Hilliard 1969). While the 1958-1960 survey project led to over 414 unique collecting events and 161 unique localities, there were 14 study sites on which the survey focused.

This map highlights fourteen of the main collecting sites surveyed by Alexander during the 1958-1960 field seasons. The locality information for Alexander’s study and other non-Alexander grasshoppers is available at the Search the Collections portion of the web site.
