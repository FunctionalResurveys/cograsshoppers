Chautauqua Mesa


photo by Don Van Horn, 1958

Elevation: 1752m (5,750ft)

Locality: A large northeast-facing meadow between the Chautauqua grounds and the entrance to Gregory Canyon to the west.

Vegetation: A mesic and bluegrass meadow, strewn with large boulders, near the edge of a coniferous forest. Vegetation is composed of grasses and forbs, with scattered clusters of low shrubby choke cherry, Prunus virginiana, and sumac, Rhus glabra. The dominant plant is the Kentucky bluegrass, Poa pratensis. Conspicuous forbs include Helianthus pumilus, Lactuca scariola and several memebers of the pea family such as Artemisia ludoviciana.
